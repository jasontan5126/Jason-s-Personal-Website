4th Post: "Red Sun" and Reflect on My Internship




July is almost coming to the end and it's almost August. It feels like this summer is going by pretty fast now that at least 1 month of my summer internship has passed by pretty fast. Before even starting the summer internship, I found myself living in a dire situation because I was living in a time where I had nobody to interact with in person other than my family members.

Until now, I felt overjoyed because of the idea that I've met a lot of new people who are mainly college students. Most of them were pretty nice. Plus they were all part of the same department that I was assigned to work for. We even sat together at a certain work table every Friday whenever it's our lunch break just so we can enjoy interacting with each other during our spare time. Not only that, I felt like I've changed ever since I first started the internship because I've learned a lot, learned to problem solve and rely on each other whenever we don't understand something to problem solve each other's problems.

While I enjoyed the summer internship, Tuesday, there was actually a rare natural phenomenon that I got the chance to see with my own eyes. This happened when I was outdoors mainly because I was taking a walk enjoying my own relaxation of nature. During that time, the sky looked so smoky even though it looked like clouds. You couldn't even breath in it because that meant damaging my respiratory system. What was interesting that I got the chance to view with my own eyes was the "red sun" high in the sky. I thought, "How could the sun be red? It's not even close to sunset or sunrise. I should expect the color of the sun to be white". Until then, I found out that it's because of the wildfire smoke from the West Coast since I initially thought there was a volcano eruption going on. I may never see this natural phenomenon in a few years.

Seeing a red sun plus doing my summer internship were my top moments this summer because it's not often that I get to experience these moments often in my life. Plus I'm hoping for good moments in the future which will bring better outcomes for me.
